How duplicate your friends

Filming time: 11 minutes
Editing time: 3 hours
Increase in Friends: 1800%

  • Things You'll Need

  • A Tripod
  • Something to film video with (Camera, iPhone, iPad, etc)
  • A tripod attachment to fit the filming device onto the tripod
  • A friend willing to be genetically cloned
  • Photoshop (or other image editing software)
  • Premiere Pro (or other video editing software)
  • 3 hours and 11 minutes to spare

  1. Obtain a Tripod

    The tripod is actually one of the most important steps here. No, propping your phone up against some books is not the same as 'getting a tripod.' A tripod allows you to keep your camera steady, so that when you go to photoshop your friend into the photo, you can be a little less precise with your masking because the backgroudns line up.

    Hey look! It's me with my tripod! I originally wanted a tripod with an iPhone stand, but I setteled with a microphone stand with an iPad holder. The important thing is that whatever you get is steady so it doesn't move during the video you record later.

  2. Film your friend

    This part is how you get the raw material for friend duplication. Once your tripod is set up and angled how you want, begin recording video on your filming device. It's recommended to start with at least one frame of the filming space being empty so you have a neutral background for later. From here, have your friend move around the area you are filming. It is imperitave that you do not touch the tripod or camera so that everything is captured at the exact same angle.

    When I was filming, I made sure do direct my friend so they were in as many different spots as possible. This way, I can clone them to my heart's content.

  3. Open the video in Adobe Premiere Pro

    Once you filmed our video, you need to get it into your video editing softwhere. I used Adobe Premiere Pro. Since I recoreded on an iPad and edited on an iMac, I was able to airdrop my video onto my computer. If you can't airdrop, you can send the video in an email, upload to something like Google Drive, or if you recoreded with an actual camera, plug in your SD card into your computer and get the file from there. After you have the file on your computer, open your editing software and open the video within it.

  4. Export the video frames you want to use

    This step is where you turn your videos into photos for Photoshop. Skim through the video in your editing software, and export any frames you want to use individually. Make sure you export a frame of the filming are being completely empty. The tutorial below explains how to do that.

  5. Photoshop out the background in one of your photos

    At this point, you should have a couple of images you exported from your video as frames. Depending on how many you have, open some or all of them in Photoshop. Assuming they opened as different files, you will need to get the photos you plan to use into 1 file. I used picture where the subject was not in the frame as my base photo during this step. I recommend doing this through copying and pasting the layer. Again, if you have a lot of files, you don't have to do this all at once.

  6. Combine your files

    Now that you have photos of your friend on a single file, you can now begin to photoshop out your friend so you can see multiple of them at once. Create a selection and mask out the background. If you have never done this in Photoshop before, here is a tutorial for selections you can use.

  7. Repeat for as many photos as you have

    This step takes the longest. For me, since I had like 18 versions of my friend, I repeated this a lot of times. Once you have repeated steps 5 and 6 as needed, export the photo, and ta-da!
